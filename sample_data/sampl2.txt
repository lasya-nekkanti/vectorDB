Role
(for 2nd-year Engineering students — all branches)
This handout simplifies the concepts from your slides into clear explanations, relatable

🌍 1. What Are Public Affairs?
In simple words:
Public Affairs means how companies or professionals interact with the public, government,
and media to influence decisions that affect society.
Why it matters for engineers:
Your projects — from roads to software — directly affect people’s safety, privacy, and
environment.
Example:
When the government plans a metro project, civil and electrical engineers share data,
design insights, and safety inputs. That’s public affairs in action.
Tip:
Think beyond your job role. Ask, “Who else will be affected by my engineering decisions?”
Reflection:
️ Who should engineers talk to before building or launching something that impacts the
public?
🧱 2. Engineers and the Public
In simple words:
Engineering isn’t just about machines or code. It’s about improving human life safely and
sustainably.
Three areas every engineer affects:
● Environment (Planet) — e.g., how much energy or waste your system uses.
● People — e.g., how safe your designs or software are.
● Profit — e.g., how responsibly a company earns money.
Example:
An app developer must consider data privacy (people), power usage (planet), and cost to
users (profit).
Tip:
Before you start any project, ask:
“Is this safe? Is this fair? Is this sustainable?”
